# The four-phase inquiry lifecycle

Polibio.ai supports the life of a field study, not a single operation. The four phases follow the researcher's own workflow; under the hood they map to the four analytical layers, but they are presented in the order you live them.

## A · Iterate hypotheses against a networked evidence base

Inductive work begins with a hunch in contact with evidence. You pose a tentative claim; the system queries the relevant sources; the claim survives, dies, or mutates; the loop runs again — in minutes, not over the weeks manual assembly would take. The discovery agents (Layer 1) carry **no theoretical vocabulary**: they surface patterns and leave interpretation for phase D, which is what keeps the induction honest.

## B · Build datasets to the study design

The most consequential inversion. Where the conventional constraint lets the available data define the answerable question, here you specify the variables, units, period, and resolution, and the evidence-constructor assembles them across sources — landing everything in a DataPool where **every observation traces to a logged retrieval** and no value is imputed without a recorded method.

## C · Prepare fieldwork by triangulating evidence, literature, and news

Before the field visit, three streams converge: the preliminary quantitative evidence, the scholarly literature (your own library searched first), and the documentary/news record. The research-designer produces a study design; the field-planner converts it into an operational manual — itinerary, archives, interview protocols keyed to the causal chain. Fieldwork itself sits here, between C and D, and it is yours: the system plans the visit, it cannot take it.

## D · Probe findings with theory, then quantitative data

Two movements, in order. First the **theory-driven** analysis (Layer 2): the field's analytical moves applied to what the fieldwork documented, each move's output constraining the next. Then **quantitative probing** (Layer 3): a claim formalised into a prediction and tested, returning a diagnosis — *consistent / inconsistent / inconclusive / surprising* — never a verdict. The probe checks whether the mechanism left a trace; the researcher decides what follows.

---

The order is not workflow convenience. It encodes an epistemology: discover and gather before interpreting; within interpretation, qualitative mechanism before quantitative test; the curated library before the comprehensive database.
